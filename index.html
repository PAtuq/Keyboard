EECS4441 Keyboard with Audial Feedback Test

Introduction

This is a web-app built to conduct experiments on the possible benefits of differentiated audio feedback in keyboards.

  1. This is a typical typing test, of the type you might find online.
  2. It will consist of 5 sentences that you must type.
  3. You will have been asked to begin either using your own, i.e., physical keyboard, or our built-in soft keyboard. If you are participating in our experiment, we ask that you use your physical keyboard
  4. Read the instructions below, and when ready to begin, click the start button to move to the test page.

Instructions for those participating in our experiment

  1. Firstly, read the general instructions above, in the introduction.
  2. You will have been asked to complete the test under one of four conditions, either:
    3. (A) : Differentiated audio feedback, forward order
    (B) : No differentiated audio feedback, forward order
    (C) : Differentiated audio feedback, reverse order
    (D) : No differentiated audio feedback, reverse order
  3. If you've been assigned to either "No-differentiated audio feedback" condition, check the "Mute audio feedback" box before starting. Or simply mute your system
  4. If you've been assigned to either reverse-order condion, check the "Reverse order" box before starting.
  5. Regardless, you will complete the test once with vision, and once with your vision of the text-input obscured.
  6. For the first test, "Blind mode" should be unchecked.
  7. For the second test, "Blind mode" should be checked.
  8. At the end of a test, you will be provided a summary of your results. Please report this summary to whoever asked you to participate (A screenshot, photo, written recordings of all values... So long as we get it)
  9. You have finished when you have completed the test twice, once with vision and once in blind-mode, and have reported to us the results. We thank you for your participation.

Some important notes

  1. The test is not case-sensitive.
  2. You will have to select, i.e., click the input-box at the very start of this test. You will not have to re-select it for every sentence, just start typing as soon as a new sentence is presented.
  3. Your speed (WPM) is tracked by a timer. This timer resets as soon as a new sentence is presented. Thereby, you are expected to complete these tests in one burst, without resting between sentences.
  4. The setting variables are stored in your browser. Thereby, when starting a new test it's possible to carry-over settings from an old test. Between your first (with-vision) and second (blind) tests, close your browser and restart the app. In a pinch, you can use ctrl + shift + i to see the console, and check/uncheck settings until you get your desired settings. In the console logs, 1 means the setting is enabled, and 0 means it is disabled.
